Virtual Assistants

Artificial intelligence is a technology field that aims to develop systems capable of performing tasks autonomously and intelligently. Present in different aspects of our daily lives, it has become essential for making processes faster, more accurate, and personalized.

Learn More

What are Virtual Assistants?

Virtual assistants are software programs designed to interact with users and help them perform tasks or obtain information in a practical and efficient way. They can understand voice or text commands, provide immediate responses, automate processes, and offer personalized support, making the digital experience more agile and intuitive.

Benefits

Time Saving

They can automate repetitive tasks, such as scheduling appointments, responding to emails, or searching for information, allowing people to focus on more important activities. Additionally, they offer immediate responses, avoiding waiting for human assistance.

24/7 Availability

They are always available, regardless of the time or day of the week. This ensures continuous support for questions, requests, or urgent problems, something that would be unfeasible with only human attendants.

Personalized and Efficient Support

They use data and interaction history to offer responses tailored to each user's profile and preferences. Thus, the service becomes faster, more assertive, and relevant, improving the user experience and satisfaction.

How They Work (Detailed)

  1. Capture:

    2. Virtual assistants receive user commands by voice (microphone) or text (keyboard).

    When the command is by voice, the audio goes through pre-processing — noise removal, volume adjustment and speech-to-text conversion through speech recognition (ASR – Automatic Speech Recognition).


  2. Natural Language Processing (NLP):

    3. After the system has the text, NLP (Natural Language Processing) comes in.

  3. In this stage, the assistant:
    • Identifies the user's intent (e.g., "play music", "schedule meeting").
    • Extracts important entities (e.g., names, dates, places, times).

    This allows it to understand the context and what the user really wants.


  4. Decision:

    5. Based on the understood information, the system decides which action to take. This decision can be guided by:

    • Programmed rules (e.g., if the command is "time", respond with the current time).
    • AI models and dialogue tools that evaluate the context and choose the best response.

  5. Action:

    6. Finally, the assistant executes the requested action:

    • Responds with synthesized voice (TTS – Text to Speech).
    • Calls APIs (such as weather forecast, calendar, music).
    • Or controls devices, such as lights, TVs, and phones.

Google Assistant

Google Assistant helps organize tasks, reminders and control smart devices in your home. It understands voice commands and can interact with various services like calendar, music and news.


Facilitates daily routine management, helping users maintain focus and efficiency in multiple tasks.

Amazon Alexa

Alexa controls smart home devices, plays music, answers questions and integrates with various services and applications, providing a connected home experience.


Centralizes control of home devices, creating automated and more comfortable environments.

Duolingo Chatbot

Duolingo's Chatbot assists in language learning, allowing practice of conversation and vocabulary in an interactive way. It simulates real dialogues to improve fluency.


Offers dynamic and accessible learning, stimulating continuous study through natural interactions.

VoiceOver / NVDA

Accessibility assistants like VoiceOver (iOS) and NVDA (Windows) help visually impaired people navigate systems and applications by reading text and describing on-screen elements.


Promotes digital inclusion by allowing visually impaired people to use technologies autonomously.
